## Membership
Two tiers. Same community.

### Free Tier
**Hackerspace access Monday – Friday, 8 AM – 5 PM. No monthly fee.**

The space, the WiFi, the coffee, the people; on us. If you're showing up during weekday hours to work, collaborate, around others with the same drive and motivation, the door is open.

**What's included:**
- Hackerspace access weekdays, 8 AM – 5 PM
- Full WiFi, workstations, and meeting space
- Unlimited coffee
- Access to all open ATC events and workshops

**What's not included:** Weekend access, after-hours access (5 PM – 8 AM).

### 24/7 Tier — $50/month
**Round-the-clock hackerspace access** for the late-night builders, weekend warriors, and "I just got hit with an idea at 2 AM" crowd.

Everything in the free tier, plus:
- 24/7 access
- Weekend access
- After-hours work without coordinating with anyone

If your best hours aren't 9 to 5, this is the tier for you.

**No contracts. No corporate BS. Just tech.**

## What It Means to Be Part of This

We're looking for people who treat ATC as a place they help *build*, not just a space they *use*. That can look like a lot of different things:

- **Helping your fellow Hackerspace members** — answering questions, pairing on problems, sharing what you know
- **Showing up to events** — Byte Night, Hack Night, workshops, the recurring beats that make this a community
- **Hosting your own events** — a workshop on your expertise, a study group, a meetup for your corner of tech
- **Being an ambassador** of a growing, thriving, serious tech community — out in the world, in the city, in your network

You don't have to do all of these. We're looking for people who show up consistently in *some* way, not people checking boxes on a contribution scorecard.

What we're *not* looking for: people who want a free coworking space, people who treat ATC like a perk to use without joining the community, or people whose only ATC interaction is benefiting from what others build.

If you're not sure whether you'd qualify, the honest answer is: apply, and let us tell you. We'd rather have the conversation than have you talk yourself out of it.

## FAQ
**Is this just for experienced developers?**

No. We welcome everyone from students to senior engineers. What matters is genuine interest in technology and building things.

**What happens to my current paid membership?**

On July 1st, your rate automatically drops from $99/month to $50/month, and your 24/7 access continues uninterrupted. If you'd prefer to switch to the free tier, message **@sly** on Discord or email **[contact email]**.

**How long does the application review take?**

We aim to respond within **[review window — TBD, e.g., two weeks]**. Reviews happen on a rolling basis, not in batches.

**What if I'm not approved?**

We'll let you know honestly and tell you what would help. Most "not yet" responses are about visibility — we don't know you yet, or we haven't seen you at events. The path forward is usually showing up to a few recurring events (Byte Night is the easiest) and reapplying.

**Can I bring guests?**

Yes. If you're a member, you can bring a guest with you — you're responsible for them while they're in the space. For repeat or extended guest access, message **@sly** so we can coordinate.

**Is there parking?**

Yes, free parking is available for all members and guests.

**What about weekends?**

Weekends are 24/7 tier only. If you only occasionally need weekend access, it's usually cheaper to upgrade for a single month than to maintain ongoing access.

**Why did you change the pricing structure?**

The short version: growing the community lets us run the building more efficiently per person. That made it possible to drop the 24/7 price and open the daytime doors to everyone who's already contributing. The longer version: we want ATC to be defined by who's in the building, not who can afford it.
